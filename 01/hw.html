A bucket

A bucket is a vessel for storing liquid and bulk materials and transporting them over short distances.

Fire buckets

Fire buckets are conical tin buckets that are convenient for pouring sand and splashing water up and forward. In addition, the conical shape prevents the use of fire buckets for domestic purposes.

Government bucket

Russian premetric unit for measuring the volume of liquids, approximately equal to 12.299 liters.

Well buckets

Well buckets have the shape of a tall, narrow cylinder, which allows the bucket to easily pass through the hatch in the upper structure of the well and makes it easier for the bucket to tip over when immersed in water.

Cylindrical buckets

Cylindrical buckets are more stable, which is a prerequisite for using them for packaging, storing and transporting paints and other chemical materials and reagents. Such buckets, as a rule, have a sealed lid, and the shape of the bottom and lid allows them to be stored in several rows.
